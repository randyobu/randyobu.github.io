Suppose that you toss a fair coin a number of times. Which of the following sequences of heads (H) and tails (T) is most likely?

  1. HHHHHTTTTT

  2. HTTHTHHHTH

  3. HTHTHTHTHT

  4. They are all equally likely.

2

Suppose someone had fallen down and was bleeding. Which of the following do you think is most likely?

  1. People in general would be most likely to help if they were the only person around.

  2. People in general would be most likely to help if there were several other people around.

3

Are there more 6 letter English words (a) ending in ‘ing’ or (b) with ‘n’ as their fifth letter?

  1. More ending in ‘ing’

  2. More with ‘n’ as their fifth letter

  3. There are the same number of each sort of word.

  4. It is impossible to tell without counting all of the words in English.

4

Which is the more likely cause of death?

  1. Homicide

  2. Suicide

5

About how many people would need to be sampled in order to make a reasonably accurate prediction (say within 5 percentage points) about who U.S. voters prefer for President (suppose that there are about 100 million voters)?

  1. About five thousand

  2. About five hundred thousand

  3. About five million

  4. More than five million

6

Subjects in an experiment were asked to administer a series of ever more painful shocks to another subject who showed up at the same time. Which of the following is more likely to be true?

  1. Most subjects would not give any shocks.

  2. Most subjects would just give a few shocks.

  3. Most subjects would give shocks but quit as soon as the other person screamed.

  4. Many subjects would continue to administer ever more painful shocks, even after the other person screamed.

7

Which of the following alternatives do you prefer?

  1. 100% chance of losing 50 dollars

  2. 25% chance of losing 200 dollars, and a 75% chance of losing nothing.

8

Of two hospitals, about 50 babies are born everyday in the larger one, and about 15 are born everyday in the smaller one. On average 50% of the babies are boys. Which hospital, if either, is more likely to have more days per year when over 65% of the babies born are boys?

  1. The big hospital

  2. The small hospital

  3. Both hospitals should be about the same.

9

Some playing cards are face down on the table. Some have red backs and others have blue backs. Which cards do you turn over to test the following claim? “If a card has an even number on front, then it has a red back.”

  1. The blue cards

  2. The red cards

  3. All the cards

10

Linda is 31 years old, single, outspoken and very bright. She majored in philosophy. As a student, she was deeply concerned with issues of discrimination and social justice, and also participated in antinuclear demonstrations. Which of the following is most likely?

  1. Linda is a bank teller.

  2. Linda is a bank teller and is active in the feminist movement.

11

Exactly 85% of the cabs in a city are blue and the other 15% are green. A witness to a hit and run accident claimed he saw a green cab. People are right under those conditions 80% of the time, but wrong 20% of the time. What is the probability that the cab was indeed a green cab, as the witness says?

  1. Very likely, almost 100%

  2. The changes are about 40%

  3. The chances are about 85%

  4. Less than 10%

12

There are three doors. Nothing is behind two of them, but 50,000 dollars behind the the remaining one. You choose a door. The host opens another door which he knows does not have the prize. He then asks if you want to stay with your original choice or switch. What should you do?

  1. Stay

  2. Switch

  3. It doesn’t matter.

13

Consider the following argument: All colas are soft drinks. All colas are beverages. Therefore, all soft drinks are beverages. Does the conclusion necessarily follow from the premises?

  1. Yes

  2. No

  3. It is impossible to tell.

14

Joe is on his way to class and will see someone slumped over in a doorway who is obviously in distress and needs help. To predict if Joe will stop and help, what do you need to know?

  1. You would need to know a good deal about Wilbur’s personality and traits to make a very good prediction.

  2. You would need to know if Wilbur was in a hurry or not.

15

Suppose that you are an excellent chess player and Susan is good, but not as good as you.

  1. You would have the best chance of beating Susan in a best of seven series

  2. You would have the best chance of beating Susan in a best of three series

  3. Your chances of beating Susan would be the same in either case.

16

A number of college students participated in an experiment. Some were asked by a very nice person to eat fried grasshoppers. Others were pressured by a rude person to eat the grasshoppers. Which group reported liking the grasshoppers?

  1. The one with the nice experimenter.

  2. The one with the rude experimenter.

  3. Both groups reported liking them about the same.

17

Which of the alternatives, 1 or 2, do you prefer?

  1. An insurance policy with a 50 dollar premium that protects you against losing 200 dollars

  2. 25% chance of losing 200 dollars, and a 75% chance of losing nothing.
